Worthless Privacy Policy

Version 1.0 · May 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the Operator, Shachar Mendelowitz, an Israeli sole proprietor based in Herzliya, Israel, handles personal data in connection with the Worthless open-source project, the wless.io website, and the Worthless GitHub repository.

1. The short version

  • Worthless is self-hosted, open-source software. The Operator does not collect, transmit, store, or process your API keys, prompt content, or response content.
  • The wless.io website is a small static site with no analytics tracking by default.
  • The GitHub repository collects whatever GitHub itself collects from visitors and contributors; that is governed by GitHub's privacy policy, not this one.
  • If you email an Operator-controlled address (legal@, privacy@, security@), your email is received and may be read.
  • If you submit the early-access waitlist form, Tally receives the information you provide and the Operator may read it.
  • That is essentially the entire data-processing scope.

2. Software (the worthless CLI and sidecar)

The Worthless Software runs on your machine and connects to LLM providers using API keys you own and pay for.

The Operator does not:

  • Receive your API keys
  • Receive prompt content, response content, or model selection
  • Operate any server through which your provider requests are proxied
  • Run telemetry or “phone home” by default

The Software may, in future versions, offer opt-in anonymous usage metrics (counts of CLI invocations, error categories, version pings). Any such feature will be:

  • Off by default
  • Documented in the release notes that introduce it
  • Disableable by a clearly documented setting

If you have not opted in, no usage data is sent to the Operator from the Software.

3. Website (wless.io)

The wless.io website is a static site published at GitHub Pages or a comparable static host.

The Operator does not place analytics scripts, advertising trackers, or third-party JavaScript by default. The hosting provider may collect standard server-log information (IP address, user agent, requested URL, timestamp) for operational and security purposes; that processing is governed by the hosting provider's own policy.

The homepage and coming-soon page may link to an optional early-access waitlist hosted by Tally. If you open or submit that form, Tally processes the information you provide under Tally's privacy policy. The Operator receives the submitted information and may use it only to evaluate interest in, and contact you about, a separate future Hosted Worthless managed product. Submitting the form is optional.

If analytics are added later, they will be:

  • Privacy-respecting (e.g., Plausible or equivalent, no cookies, no cross-site tracking)
  • Disclosed in this Policy at the time they are added

4. GitHub repository

The Worthless source code, issues, pull requests, and discussions are hosted at https://github.com/oblangatas/worthless. When you visit, file issues, open pull requests, comment, or otherwise interact with the repository, GitHub collects information about you according to GitHub's own privacy policy. The Operator does not control GitHub's data practices.

5. Email addresses

The following email addresses are operated by the Operator:

  • legal@wless.io: license inquiries, contract questions
  • privacy@wless.io: privacy inquiries, data-subject requests
  • security@wless.io: security vulnerability reports

If you email one of these addresses, your message, including any personal information you choose to include, is received by the Operator and may be read, stored, and replied to. The Operator does not commit to any response time. Responses are best effort only.

You may ask the Operator to delete your email correspondence by sending a request to privacy@wless.io. The Operator will use reasonable efforts to delete correspondence on request, subject to legal-retention obligations.

6. Legal bases (GDPR / EU and UK data subjects)

Where the General Data Protection Regulation (EU 2016/679) or the UK GDPR applies, the legal bases for the limited processing described above are:

  • Legitimate interests: operating and securing the website, responding to inbound email
  • Consent: for optional waitlist submissions and any future opt-in analytics or telemetry features
  • Compliance with legal obligations: where applicable

EU and UK data subjects have rights under GDPR / UK GDPR including access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection. The relevant statutory response window is set by GDPR Article 12 (one month, extendable where the request is complex). The Operator endeavors to comply with statutory windows where applicable but does not commit to any independent response timeframe beyond what the law requires.

To exercise these rights, contact privacy@wless.io or the EU/UK representative listed in section 11.

7. Data retention

The Operator retains only the data described above (email correspondence, optional waitlist submissions, hosting-provider server logs). Retention periods:

  • Email correspondence: retained at the Operator's discretion, deleted on request where feasible and not subject to legal-retention obligations
  • Waitlist submissions: retained while evaluating or contacting people about Hosted Worthless early access, deleted on request where feasible and not subject to legal-retention obligations
  • Server logs: governed by hosting provider's retention policy
  • GitHub data: governed by GitHub's retention policy

The Operator does not maintain a customer database, user-account system, or persistent user profiles for the open-source Software.

8. Transfers

Some hosting, email, and form providers used to operate wless.io, Operator email, and the optional waitlist may be located outside Israel or the EU/UK. Where personal data is transferred to such providers, the Operator relies on the provider's own transfer-mechanism compliance (standard contractual clauses, adequacy decisions, or equivalent). The Operator does not separately negotiate cross-border transfer mechanisms with each website visitor.

Israel is the subject of an adequacy decision by the European Commission for transfers from the EU to Israeli controllers; that adequacy decision does not, by itself, remove the Operator's other obligations under GDPR.

9. Children

The Software is intended for software developers. The Operator does not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 16. If you believe a minor has provided personal data via Operator email or the wless.io site, contact privacy@wless.io and the Operator will use reasonable efforts to delete it.

10. Security

The Operator takes reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect the limited personal data described in this Policy. However, no method of internet transmission or storage is fully secure. The Software, the website, and the Operator's email systems are provided AS IS. The Operator cannot guarantee absolute security and disclaims liability for security incidents to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law.

See the Security page for the security reporting channel.

11. EU and UK representatives

The Operator may, at its sole discretion and where required by applicable law, appoint a representative in the European Union and/or the United Kingdom pursuant to Article 27 of the GDPR and the equivalent provision of the UK GDPR.

At present, no such representative has been appointed. Worthless is open-source software for individual developer use; the Operator does not process EU/UK personal data at a scale that triggers a mandatory Article 27 appointment.

If and when a representative is appointed, the appointment will be reflected in this Policy with contact details. Until then, EU and UK data subjects may contact the Operator directly at privacy@wless.io for any inquiry related to this Privacy Policy.

EU and UK data subjects retain all rights under GDPR / UK GDPR regardless of whether a representative is currently appointed, and may file complaints with their local supervisory authority at any time.

12. Changes to this Policy

The Operator may update this Policy at any time. Updates take effect when published at wless.io/privacy. The Operator is not required to provide notice of updates.

It does not constitute a contractual commitment about future processing, and it does not modify the AS-IS disclaimers in the AGPL-3.0 license or in the Terms of Service.
